What is Magic the Gathering?

Magic: The Gathering (colloquially known as Magic cards, Magic or just MTG) is a collectible and digital collectible card game created by Richard Garfield and released by Wizards of the Coast 1993.

Each game of Magic represents a battle between wizards known as planeswalkers who cast spells, use artifacts, and summon creatures as depicted on individual cards in order to defeat their opponents, typically, but not always, by draining them of their 20 starting life points in the standard format.

Magic can be played by two or more players, either in person with printed cards or on a computer, smartphone or tablet with virtual cards through the Internet-based software such as Magic: The Gathering Arena.

Rules from and for beginners

Ways to enhance your first few hours with Magic the Gathering Arena

Rule no.1

Start playing before reading or watching too many tutorials. Alternatively watch some Let's Plays that you enjoy.

Rule no.2

Start playing with the basic standard decks, getting to know the cards. Then build your own deck based on one of those.

Rule no.3

Play whatever deck and color and strategy you enjoy.

Ignore meta.
